Scuderia Ferrari

About Ferrari

Scuderia Ferrari, founded in 1929 by Enzo Ferrari, stands as a legendary force in Formula 1, participating in every season since 1950. The team, based in Maranello, Italy, boasts a rich history, securing 16 Constructors' Championships and 15 Drivers' Championships with racing icons like Fangio, Lauda, Schumacher, and Räikkönen.

Ferrari's distinctive red cars, adorned with the prancing horse emblem, symbolize not just speed but a relentless pursuit of excellence. The passionate global fan base, known as "tifosi," further amplifies the team's iconic status.

Our current car

Ferrari recently unveiled the anticipated appearance of their SF-23 race car on the vibrant streets of Las Vegas, a city set to feature prominently in this year's racing calendar. Adding a touch of excitement to the reveal, both Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz will don specially crafted racing suits, showcasing a unique design that reflects the spirit of the occasion. This announcement not only piques the interest of motorsport enthusiasts but also adds an extra layer of anticipation for the upcoming event in Las Vegas.

Our team

The Scuderia Ferrari team typically fields two drivers in the Formula 1 championship. In recent years, as of 2021, the drivers were Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz. Leclerc, a young and promising talent, joined Ferrari in 2019, while Sainz came on board in 2021.

Both Leclerc and Sainz were expected to play crucial roles in Ferrari's pursuit of success on the Formula 1 grid. Their performances, camaraderie, and ability to push the team forward would be key elements to watch for in the evolving dynamics of Scuderia Ferrari.
